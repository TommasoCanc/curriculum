About me

I'm an ecologist with a passion for making sense of biodiversity through big data, spatial ecology, and ecological modeling. I love working with large-scale datasets to uncover patterns in species distribution and how ecosystems respond to global change.

My research explores how biodiversity is structured and how its different components like taxonomy, traits, and even genetics interact. This means I get to work with all kinds of data and tools, and I especially enjoy developing software that supports ecological research. From building databases to creating R and Python packages, I like making science more efficient and accessible.

As Head of the Data Management and Analysis Unit, I've had the chance to lead a talented, multidisciplinary team composed by biologists and software developers. I'm always looking for ways to boost collaboration, simplify complex workflows, and keep innovation at the heart of what we do.

I'm a strong believer in open science and the power of working together. Whether it's through open source tools or collaborative research, I'm committed to using good science and smart technology to help build a more sustainable future.
